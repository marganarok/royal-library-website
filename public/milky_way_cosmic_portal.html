Galaxy Structure

The Milky Way is a barred spiral galaxy containing 100-400 billion stars arranged in distinct structures. We're located about 26,000 light-years from the galactic center in the Orion Arm.

Galactic Center

Home to Sagittarius A*, a supermassive black hole 4 million times the mass of our Sun. This region is densely packed with ancient stars and intense radiation.

Central Bar

A massive stellar structure about 27,000 light-years long that extends through the galactic center. This bar helps channel gas inward to fuel star formation.

Spiral Arms

Four major arms (Perseus, Sagittarius, Centaurus, and Cygnus) where active star formation occurs. These arms contain bright, young blue stars and colorful nebulae.

Orion Arm

Our local spiral arm, also called the Local Spur. We're located between the Perseus and Sagittarius arms in this relatively minor arm structure.

Galactic Halo

A spherical region containing ancient globular clusters, individual stars, and mysterious dark matter that makes up most of the galaxy's mass.

Galactic Disk

The main flat structure containing most visible stars, about 1,000 light-years thick and 100,000 light-years in diameter. This is where we live.

Our Solar System

Our local cosmic neighborhood, where one average star (the Sun) holds eight planets, moons, asteroids, and comets in its gravitational embrace.

The Sun

A yellow dwarf star converting 600 million tons of hydrogen to helium every second. Surface temperature: 5,778K. Core temperature: 15 millionÂ°C. It contains 99.86% of the solar system's mass.

Inner Planets

Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars - rocky worlds formed from the condensation of metals and silicates close to the Sun. Earth is the only known planet with life.

Asteroid Belt

Millions of rocky remnants between Mars and Jupiter. These are building blocks that never formed a planet due to Jupiter's gravitational influence.

Gas Giants

Jupiter and Saturn - massive worlds made mostly of hydrogen and helium. Jupiter has 95 known moons and acts as a "cosmic vacuum cleaner" protecting inner planets.

Ice Giants

Uranus and Neptune - distant worlds with water, methane, and ammonia ice. Neptune has winds reaching 2,100 km/h, the fastest in the solar system.

Kuiper Belt

A region of icy bodies beyond Neptune including dwarf planets like Pluto. These frozen remnants preserve materials from the solar system's formation.
