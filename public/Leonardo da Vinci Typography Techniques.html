Leonardo's Typography Innovations:

Mirror Writing: Da Vinci wrote from right to left, creating text readable only in a mirror. This may have been natural for a left-handed writer to avoid smudging ink, but also provided secrecy for his notebooks.

Radial Arrangements: In his mechanical and anatomical drawings, text often radiates outward from central points, creating visual relationships between concepts and their physical representations.

Layered Information: Multiple text elements overlap at different angles and opacities, creating depth and showing relationships between ideas - similar to how he layered anatomical observations.

Text as Texture: Rather than just conveying information, his handwriting becomes part of the visual composition, creating texture and rhythm across the page surface.
