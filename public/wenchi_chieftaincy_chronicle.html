The Wenchi Chieftaincy Dispute: A 70-Year Chronicle

The Wenchi chieftaincy dispute represents one of Ghana's most protracted traditional authority conflicts, spanning over seven decades and involving two primary royal families: the Sofoase Yefri Royal Family (to which former Prime Minister Dr. K.A. Busia belonged) and the Ahenfie Yefri Royal Family. This dispute has been characterized by multiple government interventions, court cases, committees of inquiry, and periods of political victimization that have significantly impacted the development of the Wenchi Traditional Area.

Interactive Chronological Timeline

1949
Nana Kwame Abrefa V abdicates from the Wenchi Stool after facing seven destoolment charges, primarily instigated by the Sofoase Yefri family. On each occasion, the Asanteman Council found in his favor and dismissed the charges.
Key Figures: Nana Kwame Abrefa V (Ahenfie Yefri), Sofoase Yefri family
Source: Facts About Wenchi Stool Affairs, ModernGhana.com
1949-1950
Following Nana Kwame Abrefa V's abdication, Queen Mother Nana Gyebua Ababio (Ahenfie Yefri) initially nominated her own son as Omanhene, but this was rejected. She then nominated Dr. K.A. Busia of Sofoase Yefri as Omanhene. Nana Kusi Apea was subsequently enstooled and installed Nana Frema II (his sister) as Queen Mother - the first time the Queen Mother's stool was occupied by a member of the Sofoase Yefri family.
Key Figures: Dr. K.A. Busia, Nana Kusi Apea, Nana Frema II, Queen Mother Nana Gyebua Ababio
Source: Facts About Wenchi Stool Affairs, ModernGhana.com
1952
Jackson Commission of Inquiry established to investigate Wenchi stool affairs. This represents the first major governmental inquiry into the dispute between the two royal families.
Key Figures: Jackson Commission
Source: Facts About Wenchi Stool Affairs, ModernGhana.com
1958
Nana Kusi Apea and Nana Frema Tartuo were destooled by the Conventions People's Party (CPP) Government under Dr. Kwame Nkrumah's regime. Nana Abrefa Mbore Bediatuo VI was nominated and enstooled as the new Wenchihene. This destoolment was published in the Ghana Gazette No. 101 on November 1, 1958.
Key Figures: Dr. Kwame Nkrumah, Nana Kusi Apea, Nana Frema Tartuo, Nana Abrefa Mbore Bediatuo VI
Source: Facts About Wenchi Stool Affairs, ModernGhana.com
1966
National Liberation Council (NLC) Government enacted Chieftaincy (Amendment) Decree, 1966 (NLCD 112), which withdrew recognition of Nana Abrefa Mbore Bediatuo VI as Wenchihene and restored Nana Kusi Apea and other chiefs who had been destooled. Nana Bediatuo had ruled without complaint for 8 years.
Key Figures: National Liberation Council, Nana Abrefa Mbore Bediatuo VI, Nana Kusi Apea
Source: Facts About Wenchi Stool Affairs, ModernGhana.com
1972
General Ignatius Kutu Acheampong overthrew the government of Dr. Busia. Following this, Nana Kusi Apea and Nana Frema Tartuo suffered another removal from their positions when the new military government enacted changes affecting chieftaincy.
Key Figures: General Ignatius Kutu Acheampong, Dr. K.A. Busia, Nana Kusi Apea, Nana Frema Tartuo
Source: GhanaWeb.com
1972-1973
Chiefs and kingmakers of Wenchi, led by the Gyasewahene of Wenchi, petitioned the Government by letters dated November 6, 1972, and June 14, 1973, for the constitution of a Committee of Inquiry by the National House of Chiefs to inquire into Wenchi Stool affairs.
Key Figures: Gyasewahene of Wenchi, National House of Chiefs
Source: Facts About Wenchi Stool Affairs, ModernGhana.com
1976
National House of Chiefs Committee of Inquiry submitted its report. The Committee found that the Sofoase people were originally not a royal family but were people placed under a tree to prepare food for ancestral spirits. General Acheampong promulgated SMCD 64, under which Nana Frema Tartuo was removed as Queen Mother.
Key Figures: National House of Chiefs Committee, General Acheampong, Nana Frema Tartuo
Source: Facts About Wenchi Stool Affairs, ModernGhana.com
2003
National Reconciliation Commission (NRC), chaired by Justice Amua Sekyi, examined the Wenchi Stool Affairs case. The NRC found that the Sofoase Royal family of Busia and his siblings had been unfairly treated, concluding that the removal of Nana Kusi Apea and Nana Frema Tartuo by General Acheampong contravened democratic principles and violated their right to heritage.
Key Figures: Justice Amua Sekyi, National Reconciliation Commission
Source: ModernGhana.com - Anti-Busiaists article
September 22, 2019
Mr. Kwaku Abrefa was seen in social media pictures swearing an oath of office in his grandmother's sitting room, purporting to be the new Wenchihene. The three royal families, including his own Abusuapanin, protested against this. The Sofoase Royal family took the matter to the Judicial Committee of the Bono Regional House of Chiefs, which granted an injunction.
Key Figures: Mr. Kwaku Abrefa, Bono Regional House of Chiefs
Source: ModernGhana.com - Anti-Busiaists article
July 24, 2019
The Sofoase Yefere Royal Family filed a chieftaincy petition challenging the nomination of Mr. Abrefa Damoah as Wenchihene, citing unconstitutionality and contravention of the Chieftaincy Act, 2009 (Act 759). The Bono Regional House of Chiefs granted an injunction restraining Damoah from acting as Wenchihene.
Key Figures: Sofoase Yefere Royal Family, Mr. Abrefa Damoah, Bono Regional House of Chiefs
Source: GhanaWeb.com
July 25, 2024
The Sofoase Yefere Royal Family filed a judicial review application at the High Court in Sunyani, seeking a Mandamus order to compel the Judicial Committee of the Bono Regional House of Chiefs to constitute a panel to hear their long-standing chieftaincy petition. The application was filed through Dr. Obeng Gyan Busia, Abusuapanin of the Sofoase Yefere Royal Family.
Key Figures: Dr. Obeng Gyan Busia, High Court Sunyani, Lawyer Kwabena Asare Atuah
Source: GhanaWeb.com

  • Ancestress: Nana Asaseba Odensie
  • Historical Figures: Six children including Nana Hemaa Toa I, Nana Hene Doo, Nana Adwoa Bowi Ansa

Dossiers on Key Figures

Dr. Kofi Abrefa Busia
Former Prime Minister of Ghana (1969-1972)
  • Nominated as Omanhene of Wenchi in 1949 but declined in favor of his brother Nana Kusi Apea
  • Belonged to the Sofoase Yefri Royal Family as Abusuapanin
  • Wrote scholarly works including "The Position of Chief in Modern Ashanti Political System" (1951)
  • Claimed direct descent from the founding ancestress of the Wenchi State
  • His government was overthrown by General Acheampong in 1972, leading to renewed persecution of his family
Nana Kusi Apea
Wenchihene (Brother of Dr. K.A. Busia)
  • Accepted nomination as Wenchihene when his brother Dr. K.A. Busia declined
  • Destooled Queen Mother Nana Gyebua Ababio and installed his sister Nana Frema II
  • Made controversial decisions including appointing friends to key positions and siphoning money
  • Destooled by Nkrumah's CPP government in 1958
  • Restored by the National Liberation Council in 1966
  • Removed again during Acheampong's military government
Dr. Kwame Nkrumah
First President of Ghana
  • As leader of the CPP government, destooled Nana Kusi Apea and Nana Frema Tartuo in 1958
  • His family at Nsuaem maintains ancestry to the Ahenfie Yefri Royal House of Wenchi
  • Both he and Dr. Busia were cousins and royals of the two gates that provide occupants of the Wenchi Black Stool
  • His administration's actions initiated the pattern of political interference in Wenchi chieftaincy
General Ignatius Kutu Acheampong
Military Leader
  • Overthrew Dr. Busia's government in 1972
  • Promulgated SMCD 64 removing Nana Frema Tartuo as Queen Mother
  • His actions were later condemned by the National Reconciliation Commission as violations of democratic principles
  • Continued the pattern of political victimization against the Sofoase Yefri Royal Family
Nana Abrefa Mbore Bediatuo VI
Wenchihene (1958-1966)
  • Nominated and enstooled as Wenchihene in 1958 following Nana Kusi Apea's destoolment
  • Remained as Wenchihene for 8 years without any complaint against him
  • Properly installed according to all customary procedures required
  • Destooled by NLCD 112 in 1966 despite having caused no problems during his reign
  • No customary evidence existed for his destoolment
