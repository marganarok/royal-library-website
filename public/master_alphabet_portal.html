Historical Development

The evolution from prehistoric symbol patterns to structured alphabetic systems represents humanity's growing ability to encode abstract concepts in visual form.

100,000 - 70,000 BCE

Blombos Cave Engravings

Earliest known symbolic representations - geometric patterns suggesting rhythm, symmetry, and containment principles.

3200 BCE

Egyptian Hieroglyphs

Fully developed pictographic-phonetic system combining concrete imagery with abstract concepts.

1850 BCE

Proto-Sinaitic Script

The first alphabetic system emerges in Sinai, simplifying Egyptian concepts into 22 symbols with pictographic, phonetic, and conceptual meanings.

1200 BCE

Phoenician Alphabet

Proto-Sinaitic evolves into the Phoenician system, spreading across the Mediterranean through trade networks.

800 BCE

Greek Adaptation

Greeks adapt Phoenician letters, adding vowels and creating the template for European alphabets.

700 BCE

Etruscan Alphabet

Etruscans adapt Greek letters for their language, developing a 26-letter system with strong divinatory associations.

600 BCE

Latin Development

Romans adapt Etruscan letters, creating the foundation for modern Western alphabets.
